# workflow-dead-lead-revival.md

## Purpose

This knowledge file gives **Broker Follow-Up Machine GPT** a detailed playbook for reviving old, cold, inactive, unresponsive, or abandoned borrower leads inside a loan broker, business funding broker, or funding agency CRM.

Use this file when the GPT needs to create, audit, or improve workflows for leads that never converted, never completed an application, stopped responding early, went cold before discovery, were imported from an old spreadsheet, or were left in the CRM without a clear next action.

The goal is to help brokers recover hidden pipeline value without sounding desperate, spammy, aggressive, or careless with compliance-sensitive language.

---

## Core Principle

A dead lead is not always worthless. It is usually an old conversation with unclear timing.

Dead lead revival works best when the message is simple, respectful, and easy to answer.

Default rule:

> Do not pretend the lead is warm. Acknowledge the time gap, offer a simple reopen option, and make it easy for the borrower to say yes, no, or not now.

Bad revival language:

- “You are pre-approved.”
- “You still qualify.”
- “We can get you funded now.”
- “Last chance.”
- “You missed your opportunity.”
- “Urgent funding available.”

Better revival language:

- “We lost touch after your earlier request.”
- “If you still want to revisit it, we can pick it back up.”
- “If timing changed, no problem.”
- “Should we keep this open or close it out for now?”
- “Would it be useful to take a fresh look?”

## Dead Lead Revival vs. Other Workflows

### Dead Lead Revival

Use when the lead never meaningfully advanced or went cold before the broker had enough information.

Examples:

- New lead never responded
- Application link sent but never completed
- Old spreadsheet lead
- Paid ad lead from months ago
- Website inquiry with no conversation
- Borrower asked for info and disappeared
- Lead was imported but never contacted properly

### Stalled Deal Revival

Use when the borrower was active and then stopped moving mid-process.

### Declined Borrower Reactivation

Use when there was a prior review, not-a-fit decision, decline, or sensitive file outcome.

Important:

If the record has a prior decline, offer, lender review, underwriting discussion, or sensitive broker decision, use the declined borrower reactivation playbook instead of this dead lead revival workflow.

---

## What Counts as a Dead Lead?

A dead lead is a record that is not active and has no current next action.

Common dead lead conditions:

- No response after original follow-up
- Last touch more than 30 days ago
- No application completed
- Application abandoned
- No discovery call booked
- Discovery call no-show with no later response
- Imported list with no recent contact
- Old paid ad lead
- Old website lead
- Old referral lead never reached
- Contact attempted but never connected
- Marked cold, dormant, no response, or nurture

A dead lead should not stay in the CRM without a segment, owner, revival date, or decision.

---

## Core Dead Lead Revival Goals

A revival workflow should:

1. Reopen a low-friction conversation.
2. Identify whether the borrower still has a funding need.
3. Route interested leads back into the active pipeline.
4. Stop contacting people who are not interested or opted out.
5. Clean up CRM clutter.
6. Create a clear owner and next action for every revived lead.
7. Avoid approval, eligibility, or funding promises.

## Segmentation Strategy

Do not send the same message to every dead lead.

Segment by lead history.

### Segment 1: Old New Lead / Never Contacted

Use when the lead entered the CRM but was never reached or never properly followed up.

Best angle:

“We may have missed each other after your earlier request.”

### Segment 2: No Response After Contact Attempts

Use when the broker tried to reach the lead but never got a reply.

Best angle:

“We lost touch after your earlier request.”

### Segment 3: Application Abandoned

Use when the lead started or was sent an application but never completed it.

Best angle:

“Your earlier application was left unfinished.”

### Segment 4: Discovery No-Show

Use when the lead booked or planned a call but missed it.

Best angle:

“Looks like we missed each other last time.”

### Segment 5: Old Paid Ad Lead

Use when the lead came from an ad and may not remember the broker.

Best angle:

“You had requested information earlier.”

### Segment 6: Old Referral Lead

Use carefully because partner trust may be involved.

Best angle:

“[Partner Name] had connected us earlier.”

### Segment 7: Old Nurture Lead

Use when the borrower was not ready at the time.

Best angle:

“Checking whether timing has changed.”

---

## Timing Strategy

Use these revival timing windows:

| Lead Age | Recommended Strategy |
|---|---|
| 30-60 days old | Light revival, assume timing may have changed |
| 60-90 days old | Reopen with simple yes/no prompt |
| 90-180 days old | Fresh review invitation |
| 180-365 days old | Relationship-style check-in |
| 365+ days old | Annual / old file cleanup message |

Do not over-message very old leads. The older the lead, the softer and more context-setting the message should be.

# Standard Dead Lead Revival Sequence

## Goal

Get a simple response: yes, no, not now, or wrong person.

## Trigger

Lead is marked dormant, no response, cold, nurture, or imported old lead.

## Sequence

| Step | Timing | Channel | Goal | Owner | Stop Condition |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email | Reopen conversation | Automation/owner | Reply/opt-out |
| 2 | Day 1 | SMS | Simple yes/no prompt | Owner/automation | Reply/opt-out |
| 3 | Day 3 | Email | Offer call or next step | Owner | Reply/call booked |
| 4 | Day 5 | Call | Human touch for higher-value leads | Broker/intake | Connected |
| 5 | Day 7 | SMS | Soft close-loop | Owner/automation | Reply/opt-out |
| 6 | Day 10-14 | CRM | Archive, nurture, or schedule later | Admin/owner | Manual reopen |

---

## Day 0 Email — General Revival

**Subject:** Want to revisit this?

Hi [First Name],

We lost touch after your earlier funding request, so I wanted to check in.

If you still want to review next steps, reply here and we can pick it back up.

If timing changed, no problem — just let me know and I’ll update the file.

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

---

## Day 1 SMS — Simple Prompt

Hi [First Name], quick check-in — do you still want to revisit your earlier funding request, or should I close this out for now?

---

## Day 3 Email — Choice-Based Follow-Up

**Subject:** Should I send the next step?

Hi [First Name],

Just checking back once more.

If you still want to review funding-related next steps, I can send the application link, schedule a quick call, or update your file.

What would be easiest?

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

---

## Day 5 Call Script

Hi [First Name], this is [Sender Name] with [Company]. I’m calling because we had an earlier funding request on file and I wanted to see if you still wanted to revisit it or if timing has changed.

If you are still interested, we can confirm the next step. If not, no problem — I can update the file.

---

## Day 7 Final SMS

Hi [First Name], I’ll close the loop for now. If you want to revisit your earlier request later, just reply here and we can reopen it.

# Reply Handling

## If Lead Says “Yes”

Reply:

Great — the next step is to update the file so the team can review the current situation properly.

What has changed since your earlier request: funding need, timing, business details, or documents available?

CRM actions:

- Stop dead lead revival sequence
- Move to Re-Engaged
- Assign owner
- Create task: Update file / restart discovery
- Set due date: same day

---

## If Lead Asks “Do I qualify?”

Reply:

That needs broker review before we comment on options or next steps. I’ll flag this so the right person can take a fresh look.

CRM actions:

- Stop automation
- Add Human Review Required
- Add Broker Review Required
- Create broker review task

---

## If Lead Says “Not now”

Reply:

No problem. I’ll keep this paused for now. If timing changes later, you can reply here and we can revisit it.

CRM actions:

- Status: Not Now
- Set future nurture date
- Stop active revival sequence

---

## If Lead Says “I already got funding”

Reply:

Got it — glad you found a path forward. I’ll close this out for now. If new needs come up later, feel free to reach back out.

CRM actions:

- Status: Funded Elsewhere or Closed
- Optional future check-in date if appropriate
- Stop revival sequence

---

## If Lead Says “Stop” or Opts Out

Reply only if appropriate under the broker’s process:

Understood — we’ll stop follow-up.

CRM actions:

- Apply Do Not Contact
- Stop all outreach
- Log opt-out

---

## If Lead Says “Wrong Number”

Reply:

Sorry about that — we’ll update our records.

CRM actions:

- Mark Bad Contact Info
- Stop SMS outreach
- Create cleanup task if email exists

# Automation Map

Use this minimum viable automation for dead lead revival.

```text
Trigger: Lead enters Dead Lead Revival segment

Conditions:
- Lead is not Do Not Contact
- Lead has not opted out
- Lead has valid email or phone
- Lead has no active open deal
- Lead has no unresolved human review flag

Actions:
1. Identify lead segment
2. Assign revival owner
3. Set stage = Revival Candidate
4. Set status = Outreach Sent
5. Add tag = Dead Lead Revival
6. Send segment-specific Day 0 email
7. Wait 1 day
8. If no reply and SMS allowed, send Day 1 SMS
9. Wait 2 days
10. If no reply, send Day 3 email
11. If high-value lead, create call task
12. Wait 2-4 days
13. If no response, send final SMS/email
14. If still no response, move to Nurture, No Response, or Archive
```

# If/Then Rules

## If lead replies positively

- Stop revival sequence
- Move to Re-Engaged
- Assign broker/intake owner
- Create same-day follow-up task
- Ask for updated situation

## If lead asks about approval, qualification, or terms

- Pause automation
- Add Human Review Required
- Create broker task
- Do not answer substantively

## If lead is already active elsewhere in CRM

- Do not send revival message
- Merge or update record
- Notify current owner

## If lead came from referral partner

- Attach referral source
- Use softer language
- Do not share borrower details with partner without review

## If lead does not respond after final touch

- Stop revival sequence
- Status: No Response
- Optional: Nurture or Archive
- Keep closed reason clean

# Human Review Points

Human review is required before:

- Discussing funding terms
- Answering qualification or approval questions
- Mentioning lender fit
- Explaining prior decline or prior review result
- Interpreting documents
- Sending partner-specific borrower updates
- Reviving leads with complaints, opt-outs, or consent concerns
- Messaging imported leads with unclear consent status

Use this label:

`Human review required before sending.`

---

# Compliance and Consent Cautions

The GPT should remind users to respect:

- Opt-outs
- Do-not-contact tags
- SMS consent rules
- Email unsubscribe rules
- Wrong-number replies
- Complaint flags
- Borrower privacy
- CRM record accuracy

If consent status is unclear, the GPT should recommend a conservative approach:

- Use email before SMS if appropriate
- Keep the message informational and low-pressure
- Avoid repeated outreach
- Ask the user to confirm their outreach policy

This is operational guidance, not legal advice.

# Pipeline Audit Checklist

When auditing dead lead revival, check:

- Are dead leads segmented by source and reason?
- Are opt-outs excluded?
- Are old active opportunities excluded?
- Is there a clear owner?
- Is there a simple yes/no CTA?
- Are messages avoiding approval and eligibility claims?
- Are old referral leads handled carefully?
- Are re-engaged leads routed quickly?
- Is human review triggered for sensitive replies?
- Are no-response leads cleaned up after final touch?
- Are performance metrics tracked by source and age?

# Complete Example Workflow

## Scenario

A funding agency has 500 old paid ad leads from the last 6 months that never booked a call.

## CRM Setup

- Stage: Revival Candidate
- Status: Outreach Sent
- Priority: Low to Warm
- Tags: Dead Lead Revival, Old Paid Ad Lead, No Response Previously
- Owner: Intake rep
- Task: Monitor revival replies
- Due: Same day as campaign launch

## Sequence

| Step | Timing | Channel | Action | Owner | Stop Condition |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email | Ask if still looking | Automation | Reply/opt-out |
| 2 | Day 1 | SMS | Simple yes/no check | Automation/intake | Reply/opt-out |
| 3 | Day 3 | Email | Offer call/application next step | Automation | Reply/call booked |
| 4 | Day 5 | Call | Call high-intent responders only | Intake | Connected |
| 5 | Day 7 | SMS | Soft close-loop | Automation | Reply/opt-out |
| 6 | Day 10 | CRM | Move no-response leads to nurture/archive | Admin | Manual reopen |

## Day 0 Email

**Subject:** Still looking into funding options?

Hi [First Name],

You had requested information earlier about funding-related options, so I wanted to check whether this is still something you are looking into.

If yes, reply here and we can confirm the next step.

If not, no problem.

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## Day 1 SMS

Hi [First Name], you had requested funding info earlier. Are you still looking into this, or should I close it out for now?

## Day 3 Email

**Subject:** Should I send the next step?

Hi [First Name],

If you still want to review funding-related next steps, I can send the application link, schedule a quick call, or update your file.

What would be easiest?

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## Reply Handling

If lead says yes:

- Stop dead lead revival sequence
- Move to Re-Engaged
- Assign intake owner
- Create task: Call revived lead
- Ask what changed since the original request

If lead asks “Do I qualify?”:

- Add Human Review Required
- Create broker review task
- Reply: “That needs broker review before we comment on options or next steps. I’ll flag this so the right person can take a fresh look.”

## Next Best Action

Create a CRM revival view that excludes do-not-contact records, active deals, complaint flags, and records with unclear consent before launching the revival sequence.

# Summary Rule

Dead lead revival should cleanly reopen old conversations without pretending the lead is warm or making funding promises.

The GPT should help brokers segment old leads, send simple low-pressure messages, route positive replies quickly, stop on opt-outs, pause for sensitive funding questions, and measure whether old pipeline can be converted into active opportunities.
