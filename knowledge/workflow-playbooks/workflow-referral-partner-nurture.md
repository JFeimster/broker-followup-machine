# workflow-referral-partner-nurture.md

## Purpose

This knowledge file gives **Broker Follow-Up Machine GPT** a detailed playbook for building referral partner nurture, activation, reactivation, follow-up, and relationship management workflows for loan brokers, business funding brokers, funding agencies, and broker teams.

Use this file when the GPT needs to create, audit, or improve workflows for referral sources such as CPAs, accountants, realtors, consultants, business coaches, marketing agencies, ISOs, funding brokers, insurance agents, attorneys, payroll providers, bookkeeping firms, local business groups, and other professionals who may refer borrowers or business owners.

The goal is to help brokers create a repeatable referral partner system that produces more warm introductions, protects partner trust, follows up consistently, and avoids sloppy handoffs.

---

## Core Principle

Referral partners are not lead vendors. They are relationship assets.

A partner nurture workflow should make the partner feel:

- Remembered
- Respected
- Useful
- Informed at the right level
- Clear on how to refer
- Confident that referred clients will be handled professionally

Default rule:

> The broker’s job is to make referring easy, safe, and relationship-positive.

A referral partner workflow should never pressure partners, over-message them, share private borrower details casually, or treat every partner the same.

---

## Referral Partner Nurture Goals

A strong referral partner workflow should:

1. Identify potential referral partners.
2. Start conversations with a clear value proposition.
3. Explain what makes a good referral.
4. Make the referral process simple.
5. Acknowledge referred leads quickly.
6. Keep partners updated without violating borrower privacy.
7. Follow up consistently with useful reminders and value.
8. Reactivate inactive partners respectfully.
9. Track partner activity, quality, and relationship health.
10. Assign owners and next actions so no partner relationship goes cold.

## Referral Partner Types

Broker Follow-Up Machine should adapt nurture messaging based on partner type.

### CPA / Accountant

Likely cares about:

- Client cash flow
- Tax season timing
- Working capital needs
- Responsible referrals
- Professional reputation

Best angle:

“Help business clients understand next-step funding options when cash flow or growth needs come up.”

### Realtor / Real Estate Professional

Likely cares about:

- Deals closing
- Investor clients
- Self-employed buyers
- Business owners needing liquidity
- Property-related timelines

Best angle:

“Support business-owner clients or investors who need capital conversations handled quickly and professionally.”

### Business Consultant / Coach

Likely cares about:

- Client growth
- Operations
- Cash flow
- Strategic expansion
- Trusted resources

Best angle:

“Give clients a reliable funding review resource when capital becomes part of the growth plan.”

### Marketing Agency / Service Provider

Likely cares about:

- Clients needing budget for campaigns, inventory, hiring, or expansion
- Helping clients keep projects moving
- Retaining client trust

Best angle:

“Help clients explore funding options when cash flow is holding back growth initiatives.”

### ISO / Funding Industry Partner

Likely cares about:

- Deal placement
- Speed
- Communication
- Revenue share or referral economics, if applicable
- Clear submission process

Best angle:

“Create a clean handoff and follow-up process so referred files do not get lost.”

### Attorney / Professional Advisor

Likely cares about:

- Professionalism
- Careful communication
- Client confidentiality
- Clear boundaries

Best angle:

“Provide a professional review path for clients who need business funding conversations handled carefully.”

### Bookkeeper / Payroll Provider

Likely cares about:

- Client cash flow
- Payroll crunches
- Seasonal working capital
- Business stability

Best angle:

“Give business clients a next-step resource when cash flow, payroll, or timing becomes a concern.”

## Relationship Ownership

### Referral Manager

Owns:

- Partner pipeline
- Outreach cadence
- Partner follow-up
- Partner education
- Partner reactivation
- Partner performance tracking

### Broker / Agency Owner

Owns:

- Strategic partner relationships
- High-value referral sources
- Sensitive relationship issues
- Revenue share or commercial discussions
- Partner complaints
- Escalations

### Intake / Processor

Owns:

- Acknowledging referred leads
- Updating internal CRM records
- Making sure referred borrowers are contacted quickly
- Confirming referral source is attached to lead

### VA / Admin

Can own:

- Routine nurture tasks
- CRM cleanup
- Follow-up scheduling
- Sending approved partner materials
- Reminders

Should not own:

- Sensitive partner negotiations
- Borrower-specific confidential updates
- Complaints
- Funding terms or borrower outcome explanations

### Solo Broker Version

For a solo broker, treat partner management as four operating modes:

- **Relationship Mode:** outreach, calls, trust-building, partner check-ins
- **Admin Mode:** CRM updates, tasks, reminders, tracking
- **Referral Intake Mode:** acknowledge and process referred leads
- **Review Mode:** pause before partner compensation, sensitive borrower details, complaint handling, or borrower outcome discussions

## Partner Nurture Philosophy

Referral partner nurture should be useful, not needy.

Bad partner nurture says:

“Send me leads.”

Good partner nurture says:

“Here is how to spot a client who may need help, and here is an easy way to introduce them.”

The best nurture messages usually include one of these:

- A clear referral trigger
- A short client scenario
- A useful question to ask clients
- A simple referral process
- A reminder of who is a good fit
- A quick check-in
- A relationship-building touch
- A success story without private details
- A seasonal business reason to reconnect

# Partner Lifecycle Workflow

## Stage 1: Partner Prospect

### Goal

Identify and qualify potential referral partners.

### Trigger

A potential partner is added from networking, LinkedIn, local business groups, existing client relationships, cold outreach, or manual research.

### CRM Setup

- Stage: Partner Prospect
- Status: Needs Outreach
- Tag: Partner Prospect + partner type
- Owner: Referral manager or broker
- Task: Send initial partner outreach

### Qualification Questions

- Do they serve business owners?
- Do their clients experience cash flow or growth funding needs?
- Is there relationship overlap?
- Are they trusted by their clients?
- Would a referral relationship be brand-safe?
- Is the partner likely to send quality introductions?

---

## Stage 2: Contacted

### Goal

Start a simple conversation.

### Trigger

Initial outreach is sent.

### CRM Action

- Set status: Outreach Sent
- Create follow-up task due in 3-5 business days
- Log channel used

### Initial Outreach Email

**Subject:** Quick referral question

Hi [Partner Name],

I noticed you work with business owners, and I wanted to reach out.

We help business owners review funding-related next steps when cash flow, growth, inventory, payroll, or expansion needs come up.

If you ever have a client who needs a professional funding review, I’d be happy to be a resource.

Would it make sense to connect for a quick intro?

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

### Initial LinkedIn / Short Message

Hi [Partner Name], I saw that you work with business owners. I help with funding review conversations when clients are dealing with cash flow, growth, or expansion needs. Open to a quick connection?

---

## Stage 3: Conversation Started

### Goal

Clarify fit and explain the referral process.

### Call Agenda

1. Learn who the partner serves.
2. Explain what types of client situations are worth reviewing.
3. Explain how referrals are handled.
4. Set expectations around communication and privacy.
5. Offer a simple referral path.
6. Schedule next touch or send referral instructions.

### Call Script

Thanks for taking the time. The main reason I wanted to connect is that many business owners do not know what to do when cash flow, expansion, inventory, payroll, or timing issues come up. We help review the situation and point them toward the next step when appropriate.

The easiest referral is usually a quick intro email or form submission. Once we receive it, we follow up quickly and keep the process organized.

### Discovery Questions for Partner

- What types of clients do you usually work with?
- Do your clients ever ask about funding, cash flow, or expansion capital?
- What situations usually trigger that conversation?
- How do you prefer to make introductions?
- What would make the referral process easy for you?

---

## Stage 4: Meeting Scheduled

### Goal

Make sure the partner meeting happens and is productive.

### Reminder Email

**Subject:** Quick reminder for our call

Hi [Partner Name],

Looking forward to our call on [Date/Time].

I’ll keep it simple — I’d like to learn more about the clients you serve and share when it may make sense to send someone our way for a funding review.

Talk soon,
[Sender Name]

### No-Show Follow-Up

Hi [Partner Name], looks like we missed each other. No worries. Would you like to reschedule, or should I send a quick overview by email instead?

---

## Stage 5: Partner Active

### Goal

Give the partner a clear, easy way to refer.

### Trigger

Partner agrees to collaborate, requests referral instructions, or sends a lead.

### CRM Setup

- Stage: Partner Active
- Status: Active Partner
- Tag: Partner Active
- Task: Send referral process

### Referral Process Email

**Subject:** Easy referral process

Hi [Partner Name],

Here’s the easiest way to send someone over:

1. Make a quick intro by email or text.
2. Include the client’s name, business name, and best contact info.
3. Let them know we’ll reach out to review the next step.

Simple intro language you can use:

“I wanted to introduce you to [Broker Name]. They help business owners review funding-related next steps when cash flow, growth, or timing needs come up. I’ll let you two connect from here.”

Once we receive the referral, we’ll follow up quickly and handle the conversation professionally.

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

---

## Stage 6: Lead Sent

### Goal

Acknowledge the partner quickly and protect the referral relationship.

### Trigger

Partner sends a borrower lead.

### SLA

Acknowledge partner within 15 minutes when possible.
Contact borrower within 5 minutes when possible.

### Partner Acknowledgment

Hi [Partner Name], thanks for sending [Client First Name] over. We received the referral and will follow up shortly. I’ll keep you posted as appropriate.

### Borrower Intro SMS

Hi [Client First Name], this is [Broker Name] with [Company]. [Partner Name] suggested we connect. What’s the best time today for a quick call?

### Privacy Rule

Do not share sensitive borrower details with the partner unless the user confirms it is appropriate and reviewed.

Safe partner updates are generic:

- “We received the referral.”
- “We reached out.”
- “We connected with them.”
- “We’ll keep the process moving.”

Unsafe partner updates include:

- Funding amount
- Terms
- Rate
- Documents
- Decline reason
- Credit or underwriting details
- Sensitive business information

---

## Stage 7: Partner Nurture

### Goal

Stay top-of-mind without asking for referrals every time.

### Recommended Cadence

| Partner Type | Suggested Cadence |
|---|---:|
| Strategic / high-value partner | Every 2-4 weeks |
| Active partner | Monthly |
| Warm partner | Every 30-45 days |
| Low-activity partner | Quarterly |
| Inactive partner | Reactivation every 60-90 days |

### Nurture Themes

Rotate these themes:

1. Referral trigger reminder
2. Client scenario
3. Useful question to ask clients
4. Simple referral process reminder
5. Seasonal cash flow note
6. Partner check-in
7. Anonymous success-style story
8. Educational tip
9. Reconnection message
10. Appreciation message

# Inactive Partner Reactivation

## Trigger

Partner has not replied, sent a lead, attended a meeting, or engaged for 60-90 days.

## Goal

Restart the relationship without guilt or pressure.

## CRM Setup

- Stage: Partner Reactivation
- Status: Reactivation Due
- Tags: Partner Inactive, Partner Reactivation
- Task: Send partner reactivation message

## Email Template

**Subject:** Still a useful resource?

Hi [Partner Name],

It has been a little while, so I wanted to check in.

Is it still useful for me to be a resource when clients ask about cash flow, funding, growth, or timing issues?

If yes, happy to stay connected. If not, no problem — just let me know and I’ll stop checking in.

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## SMS Template

Hi [Partner Name], quick check-in — is it still useful for me to be a resource when clients ask about funding or cash flow? If not, no problem.

## Final Soft Touch

Hi [Partner Name], I’ll pause the check-ins for now. If a client ever needs a funding review resource, feel free to reach out anytime.

# Partner Lead Handoff Workflow

## Trigger

Partner submits a borrower lead.

## Goal

Contact the borrower quickly and reassure the partner that the handoff was received.

## Workflow

| Step | Timing | Channel | Action | Owner | Stop Condition |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Immediate | CRM | Create borrower record and attach partner | Automation/Admin | Duplicate/invalid lead |
| 2 | 0-15 min | SMS/email | Acknowledge partner | Broker/intake | Partner acknowledged |
| 3 | 0-5 min | Call/SMS | Contact borrower | Broker/intake | Borrower reached/opt-out |
| 4 | Same day | CRM | Create next task | Owner | Task completed |
| 5 | 24-48 hrs | Partner update | Generic status update if appropriate | Broker/referral manager | Update sent |

## Partner Acknowledgment Email

**Subject:** Got the referral

Hi [Partner Name],

Thanks for sending [Client First Name] over. We received the referral and will follow up with them shortly.

We’ll keep the process organized and update you as appropriate.

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## Generic Partner Update

## Privacy Warning

The GPT should always avoid detailed borrower updates unless the user confirms the partner is allowed to receive them and the message has been reviewed.

# Partner Objection Handling

## “I do not want to send clients into a sales pitch.”

Completely understand. The goal is a professional review conversation, not a pressure pitch. If someone is not a fit or not ready, the conversation should still be handled respectfully.

## “How do I know my client will be treated well?”

That is exactly why we keep the handoff simple and organized. We follow up quickly, keep the conversation clear, and avoid making promises or pressure-based claims.

## “What kind of clients should I send?”

Good referrals are usually business owners dealing with cash flow timing, growth expenses, inventory, payroll, expansion, or a short-term funding question. They do not need to know the exact solution before they talk with us.

## “Do you pay referral fees?”

That depends on the broker’s approved process and any applicable rules. This should be handled by the broker or agency owner before anything is promised.

## “Can you update me on what happened with my client?”

We can share general process updates where appropriate, but we need to be careful with client-specific details. A broker should review what can be shared.

# If/Then Rules

## If partner replies positively

- Move to Conversation Started or Partner Active
- Create follow-up task
- Send referral process
- Ask preferred referral method

## If partner sends a lead

- Acknowledge partner quickly
- Create borrower record
- Attach partner source
- Start new lead workflow
- Send only generic partner updates unless reviewed

## If partner asks about referral compensation

- Pause automation
- Assign broker or agency owner
- Do not promise referral fees automatically

## If partner asks for borrower-specific details

- Pause and require human review
- Share only generic updates unless approved

## If partner stops engaging

- Move to Partner Reactivation
- Send soft reactivation message
- If no response, reduce cadence

## If partner asks not to be contacted

- Apply Do Not Contact
- Stop partner nurture
- Log request

# Human Review Points

Human review is required before:

- Discussing referral fees or compensation
- Sharing borrower-specific updates
- Discussing funding terms with a partner
- Explaining borrower decline or outcome
- Mentioning lender-specific details
- Handling partner complaints
- Sending co-branded messaging
- Making promises about speed, approval, or funding outcomes
- Discussing confidential borrower documents or financial details

Use this phrase:

> “This should be reviewed by the broker or relationship owner before sending.”
