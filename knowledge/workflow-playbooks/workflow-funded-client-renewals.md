# workflow-funded-client-renewals.md

## Purpose

This knowledge file gives **Broker Follow-Up Machine GPT** a detailed playbook for building funded-client renewal, repeat funding, post-funding check-in, retention, and referral workflows for loan brokers, business funding brokers, funding agencies, and broker teams.

Use this file when the GPT needs to create, audit, or improve workflows for clients who have already funded, completed a transaction, accepted an offer, or finished a broker-led funding process and may need future capital, renewal review, additional funding review, referral follow-up, or relationship maintenance.

The goal is to help brokers turn funded clients into repeat clients and referral sources without implying eligibility, approval, pre-approval, guaranteed renewal, or lender-specific outcomes.

---

## Core Principle

A funded client is not the end of the pipeline. It is the beginning of the retention pipeline.

Most brokers lose repeat revenue because they only follow up when they remember to. A funded-client workflow should make repeat follow-up predictable, respectful, and useful.

Default rule:

> Renewal outreach should invite a fresh review, not imply the client is eligible, approved, pre-approved, or guaranteed to receive additional funding.

Do not say:

- “You qualify for renewal.”
- “You are pre-approved for more capital.”
- “You can get funded again.”
- “You are eligible now.”
- “We can renew you today.”
- “You are approved for additional funding.”

Use language like:

- “It may be worth reviewing your current needs.”
- “If your business has new plans or expenses, we can take a fresh look.”
- “Would you like to review whether there is a sensible next step?”
- “A broker should review your current file before any options are discussed.”

---

## Why Funded-Client Renewal Workflows Matter

Funded clients are often the highest-leverage segment in a broker database because they already know the broker, have completed a process before, and may need additional capital later.

A good renewal workflow can help:

- Increase repeat funding opportunities
- Reduce one-and-done client loss
- Create review and referral opportunities
- Improve client experience after funding
- Prevent competitors from winning the next funding need
- Build predictable retention follow-up
- Create a reason to check in without sounding salesy
- Keep CRM records current

Renewal workflows should be relationship-first and review-conscious.

---

## What Counts as a Funded Client Renewal Workflow?

Use this workflow when a client has already funded or completed a transaction and the broker wants to create future follow-up around:

- Post-funding check-ins
- Satisfaction follow-up
- Review requests
- Referral requests
- 30-day check-ins
- 60-day check-ins
- 90-day renewal review prompts
- 180-day relationship check-ins
- Annual business funding reviews
- Additional capital conversations
- Repeat funding opportunities
- Partner/referral loop updates

This workflow can also apply to clients who were funded elsewhere if the broker wants a soft future check-in, but the messaging should be adjusted.

## Timing Strategy

Use these timing targets unless the user provides different standards.

| Timing | Workflow Purpose | Primary Goal |
|---|---|---|
| 3-7 days after funding | Post-funding check-in | Confirm experience and answer basic next-step questions |
| 14 days after funding | Relationship touch | Make sure client is settled and supported |
| 30 days after funding | Early check-in | Ask how things are going and identify future timing |
| 60 days after funding | Soft review prompt | Ask if new needs are coming up |
| 90 days after funding | Renewal / re-review prompt | Invite fresh review if appropriate |
| 180 days after funding | Relationship / growth check-in | Reconnect and identify changes |
| 365 days after funding | Annual review | Ask about new plans, expenses, or funding needs |

Important:

These are operational timing examples, not a claim that a client is eligible for renewal at any specific time.

---

## Human Review Requirement

Human review is required before:

- Saying a client is eligible for renewal
- Saying a client qualifies for additional funding
- Mentioning specific offers, rates, fees, terms, payment amounts, or timelines
- Comparing renewal options
- Discussing lender-specific programs
- Interpreting past performance, repayment history, bank statements, or financial documents
- Saying the client can or cannot receive more funding
- Making recommendations about whether the client should take additional funding

The GPT may draft general check-ins and fresh-review invitations. Anything specific to eligibility, offers, terms, lender options, or underwriting must be labeled:

> Human review required before sending.

# Renewal Workflow Architecture

A complete funded-client workflow has five layers:

1. **Post-funding care** — confirm the client experience and next steps.
2. **Relationship maintenance** — stay useful without over-contacting.
3. **Renewal watch** — track timing and future needs.
4. **Fresh review invitation** — invite a current review using safe language.
5. **Re-engagement routing** — move interested clients back into the active borrower pipeline with owner, task, and review rules.

# Post-Funding Check-In Workflow

## Goal

Make sure the client feels supported after funding and create a positive handoff into retention.

## Trigger

Deal is marked `Funded`.

## Timing

3-7 days after funding.

## CRM Setup

- Stage: Post-Funding Check-In
- Status: Check-In Due
- Tags: Funded Client, Post-Funding Check-In
- Owner: Broker or retention owner
- Task: Post-funding check-in
- Due: 3-7 days after funding

## Email Template

**Subject:** Quick check-in after funding

Hi [First Name],

I wanted to check in now that your funding process is complete.

How is everything going so far?

If you have any questions about next steps or need anything from our side, reply here and we’ll point you in the right direction.

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## SMS Template

Hi [First Name], quick check-in now that your funding process is complete — how is everything going so far?

## Call Opener

Hi [First Name], this is [Sender Name] with [Company]. I’m just checking in after your funding to make sure everything went smoothly and see if you had any follow-up questions.

## CRM Action

- Log response
- Set next check-in date
- Add renewal watch date
- Add review/referral request task if appropriate

# Review and Referral Request Workflow

## Goal

Ask for a review or referral after a positive client experience.

## Trigger

Client funded and had a positive post-funding check-in.

## Timing

7-21 days after funding, depending on relationship and business process.

## Important Rule

Do not ask for referrals if the client is confused, unhappy, has a complaint, or needs unresolved support.

## Review Request Email

**Subject:** Quick favor if the process helped

Hi [First Name],

I’m glad we were able to help with the process.

If the experience was helpful, would you be open to leaving a quick review or sharing feedback?

[Review Link]

Thanks again,
[Sender Name]

## Referral Request Email

**Subject:** Know another business owner who may need help?

Hi [First Name],

If you know another business owner who is looking into funding options, feel free to send them our way.

We’re happy to take a look and point them toward the right next step.

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## SMS Referral Ask

Hi [First Name], glad we could help. If you know another business owner who wants to review funding options, feel free to send them my way.

## CRM Action

- Add tag: Review Request Sent or Referral Request Sent
- Create follow-up task if client replies
- Track referrals by client source

# 30-Day Check-In Workflow

## Goal

Stay connected shortly after funding without pushing another transaction too early.

## Trigger

30 days after funding.

## CRM Setup

- Stage: Renewal Watch
- Status: Check-In Due
- Tags: 30-Day Check-In, Funded Client
- Owner: Retention owner or broker

## Email Template

**Subject:** 30-day check-in

Hi [First Name],

Just checking in about a month after your funding.

How are things going with the business since then?

No pressure on anything — I just like to check in and make sure clients are in good shape after the process.

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## SMS Template

Hi [First Name], checking in about a month after funding — how are things going with the business so far?

## CRM Action

- Log response
- If client mentions new needs, create broker review task
- If no response, keep in renewal watch
- Set 60- or 90-day follow-up

# 60-Day Soft Review Workflow

## Goal

## Trigger

60 days after funding.

## CRM Setup

- Status: Check-In Due
- Tags: 60-Day Check-In, Renewal Candidate

## Email Template

**Subject:** Any upcoming funding needs?

Hi [First Name],

I wanted to check in and see how things are going since your last funding.

Do you have any new plans, expenses, or upcoming needs that may be worth reviewing?

If not, no problem — I can check back later.

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## SMS Template

Hi [First Name], quick check-in — any new plans or upcoming funding needs worth reviewing, or should I check back later?

## Human Review Note

If the client asks whether they can renew, qualify, or receive additional funding, route to broker review.

# 90-Day Renewal Review Workflow

## Goal

Invite a fresh review if appropriate while avoiding renewal promises.

## Trigger

90 days after funding or at the user-defined renewal review date.

## CRM Setup

- Stage: Renewal Review Due
- Status: Re-Review Due
- Tags: 90-Day Review, Renewal Candidate, Broker Review Required
- Owner: Broker or retention owner
- Task: Review funded client for renewal outreach

## Email Template

**Subject:** Should we take a fresh look?

Hi [First Name],

It has been a little while since your last funding.

If your business has new plans, upcoming expenses, or changing cash flow needs, it may be worth taking a fresh look at your current situation.

Would you like to review the next step?

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## SMS Template

Hi [First Name], it has been a little while since your last funding. If your business has new needs, would you like to take a fresh look?

## Broker Call Opener

Hi [First Name], this is [Broker Name] with [Company]. I’m checking in because it has been a while since your last funding, and I wanted to see if your business has any new plans or upcoming needs worth reviewing.

## Avoid

- “You are eligible now.”
- “You qualify for renewal.”
- “We can renew you.”
- “You are approved for more.”

# Standard Funded Client Renewal Sequence

Use this sequence after the client reaches the renewal review window.

| Step | Timing | Channel | Goal | Owner | Stop Condition |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email/SMS | Invite fresh review | Retention owner | Reply/opt-out |
| 2 | Day 2 | SMS | Simple yes/no prompt | Owner/automation | Reply/opt-out |
| 3 | Day 5 | Email | Offer call or updated review | Owner | Reply/call booked |
| 4 | Day 7 | Call | Human touch | Broker/owner | Connected |
| 5 | Day 10 | SMS | Soft close-loop | Owner/automation | Reply/opt-out |
| 6 | Day 14 | CRM | Return to renewal watch | Admin/owner | Manual reopen |

## Day 2 SMS

Hi [First Name], quick follow-up — do you want to review whether there is a next step, or should I check back later?

## Day 5 Email

**Subject:** Should I check back later?

Hi [First Name],

Just checking back.

If you want to review current needs, we can schedule a quick call or update the file.

If now is not the right time, no problem — reply “later” and I’ll check back another time.

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## Day 10 SMS

Hi [First Name], I’ll close the loop for now. If you want to review future funding needs later, just reply here and we can pick it back up.

# Segment-Specific Renewal Playbooks

## Segment: High-Value Funded Client

### Strategy

Use more personal broker touches and less automation.

### Sequence

- 3-7 days: Broker check-in
- 30 days: Relationship check-in
- 60-90 days: Broker review task before outreach
- Renewal window: Personal call + reviewed message

### Message

Hi [First Name], this is [Broker Name]. I wanted to personally check in and see how things are going since your last funding. If your business has new plans or changing needs, we can take a fresh look when it makes sense.

---

## Segment: Funded Client From Referral Partner

### Strategy

Support the client while protecting confidentiality and partner trust.

### Client Message

Hi [First Name], checking in since your funding process is complete. How are things going so far?

### Partner Update

Hi [Partner Name], quick update — we were able to complete the process with [Client First Name]. Thanks again for the referral. We’ll continue to support them as appropriate.

### Privacy Rule

Do not share terms, funding amount, documents, repayment details, decline reasons, or sensitive borrower information with referral partners unless the user confirms it is appropriate and reviewed.

---

## Segment: Seasonal Business

### Strategy

Align outreach with predictable business cycles.

### Message

Hi [First Name], I know your business may have seasonal planning needs. If you have upcoming expenses, inventory, staffing, or cash flow changes, we can take a fresh look and confirm the next step.

### CRM Note

Set renewal review dates around the client’s seasonal buying, inventory, hiring, or cash flow cycle.

---

## Segment: Client Funded Elsewhere After Working With Broker

### Strategy

Stay relationship-friendly without sounding possessive.

### Message

Hi [First Name], checking in to see how things are going since your last funding process. If you have new or upcoming needs later, happy to help you review the next step.

---

## Segment: Repeat Client Already Re-Engaged

### Strategy

Move from renewal outreach to active borrower workflow.

### CRM Action

- Stop renewal sequence
- Stage: Connected / Discovery Started or Application Started
- Status: Re-Engaged
- Owner: Broker
- Task: Update file for fresh review
- Human review: Required before terms, offers, or lender-specific discussion

---

# Reply Handling

## If Client Says “Yes, I want to review options”

Reply:

Great — the next step is to update the file so the team can review your current situation properly.

Has anything changed since the last funding, such as funding need, timing, revenue, expenses, or documents available?

Then:

- Stop renewal sequence
- Move to Renewal Conversation Started
- Create broker review task
- Assign owner

## If Client Asks “Am I eligible for renewal?”

Reply:

That needs broker review before we comment on options or next steps. I’ll flag this so the right person can take a fresh look.

Then:

- Add Human Review Required
- Add Broker Review Required
- Create broker task
- Stop automation

## If Client Says “Not now”

Reply:

No problem. I’ll check back another time unless you reach out sooner.

Then:

- Status: Not Now
- Set future follow-up date
- Keep in Renewal Watch

## If Client Asks About Rates, Terms, or Payments

Reply:

A broker should review your current file before discussing any terms. I’ll flag this for review.

Then:

- Add Human Review Required
- Create broker task
- Stop automation

## If Client Opts Out

Then:

- Stop all sequences
- Apply Do Not Contact
- Log opt-out
- Do not continue outreach

# Automation Map

Use this minimum viable funded-client renewal automation.

```text
Trigger: Deal marked Funded

Actions:
1. Set lifecycle/status = Funded Client
2. Add tag = Funded Client
3. Create post-funding check-in task due in 3-7 days
4. Create optional review/referral request task due in 7-21 days
5. Set 30-day check-in date
6. Set 60-day check-in date
7. Set 90-day renewal review date, if appropriate
8. Set 180-day relationship check-in date
9. Set annual review date
10. Assign retention owner
11. Stop active borrower follow-up sequences
12. Start funded-client retention workflow
```

```text
Trigger: Renewal review date reached

Conditions:
- Contact is not Do Not Contact
- Contact has not opted out
- Client has valid contact info
- No active open deal already exists

Actions:
1. Create task = Review funded client before outreach
2. Notify broker/retention owner
3. If safe, send fresh review invitation
4. Wait 2 days
5. If no reply, send short SMS check-in
6. Wait 3 days
7. If no reply and high value, create call task
8. If reply, stop sequence and move to Renewal Conversation Started
9. If no response after final touch, return to Renewal Watch
```

# If/Then Rules

## If client replies positively

- Stop renewal automation
- Move to Renewal Conversation Started
- Create broker review task
- Ask for updated details
- Do not discuss terms until review

## If client is already in active deal pipeline

- Do not send renewal outreach
- Add note to active deal
- Let current owner handle follow-up

## If client asks about eligibility or pre-approval

- Pause automation
- Add Human Review Required
- Create broker task
- Use safe response

## If client asks for funding amount or payment estimates

- Pause automation
- Add Broker Review Required
- Do not estimate

## If client says timing is later

- Set future follow-up date
- Keep in Renewal Watch
- Do not keep pushing

## If client is unhappy or complains

- Stop renewal ask
- Create manager/broker review task
- Do not ask for review or referral

# Pipeline Audit Checklist

When auditing funded-client renewals, check:

- Are funded clients moved into a retention workflow?
- Is a post-funding check-in task created?
- Is a renewal review date set?
- Is there a retention owner?
- Are clients tagged as Funded Client / Renewal Candidate?
- Are review and referral requests only sent after positive experiences?
- Are renewal messages avoiding eligibility and approval claims?
- Are high-value clients receiving personal broker touches?
- Are clients with active deals excluded from renewal automation?
- Are opt-outs excluded?
- Are referral partner updates privacy-safe?
- Are replies routed to a broker or retention owner?
- Are repeat funding results tracked?

# Complete Example Workflow

## Scenario

A business funding client funded 90 days ago. The broker wants a safe renewal review workflow.

## CRM Setup

- Stage: Renewal Review Due
- Status: Re-Review Due
- Priority: Warm
- Tags: Funded Client, Renewal Candidate, 90-Day Review, Broker Review Required
- Owner: Retention owner
- Broker review owner: Broker
- Task: Review funded client for renewal outreach
- Due: Today
- Renewal review date: 90 days after funding

## Human Review Requirement

Human review is required before discussing renewal eligibility, offers, funding amount, rates, terms, lender fit, or updated file quality.

## Sequence

| Step | Timing | Channel | Action | Owner | Stop Condition |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | CRM | Broker/owner reviews client record | Broker/owner | Approved to outreach |
| 2 | Day 0 | Email/SMS | Invite fresh review | Owner | Reply/opt-out |
| 3 | Day 2 | SMS | Ask whether to review or check back later | Automation/owner | Reply/opt-out |
| 4 | Day 5 | Email | Offer call or updated file review | Owner | Call booked/reply |
| 5 | Day 7 | Call | Personal check-in | Broker | Connected |
| 6 | Day 10 | SMS | Soft close-loop | Automation/owner | Reply/opt-out |
| 7 | Day 14 | CRM | Return to renewal watch | Admin/owner | Manual reopen |

## Day 0 Email

**Subject:** Should we take a fresh look?

Hi [First Name],

It has been a little while since your last funding.

If your business has new plans, upcoming expenses, or changing cash flow needs, it may be worth taking a fresh look at your current situation.

Would you like to review the next step?

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## Day 0 SMS

Hi [First Name], it has been a little while since your last funding. If your business has new needs, would you like to take a fresh look?

## Reply Handling

If client says yes:

- Stop renewal sequence
- Move to Renewal Conversation Started
- Create broker review task
- Ask what has changed since last funding
- Do not discuss eligibility, rates, terms, or options until broker review

## Safe Response to Eligibility Question

That needs broker review before we comment on options or next steps. I’ll flag this so the right person can take a fresh look.

## Next Best Action

Create an automation that marks every funded client with a renewal review date, assigns a retention owner, and creates a broker review task before any renewal-specific outreach is sent.

# Summary Rule

A funded-client renewal workflow should turn completed deals into long-term relationships without implying eligibility, approval, pre-approval, or guaranteed future funding.

The GPT should help brokers create respectful check-ins, review invitations, referral opportunities, and retention systems that route interested clients back into the active pipeline with a clear owner, next action, human review point, and safe language.
