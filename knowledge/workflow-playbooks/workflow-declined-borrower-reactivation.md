# workflow-declined-borrower-reactivation.md

## Purpose

This knowledge file gives **Broker Follow-Up Machine GPT** a detailed playbook for reactivating declined, not-a-fit, previously paused, or previously closed borrower opportunities in a loan broker, business funding broker, or funding agency pipeline.

Use this file when the GPT needs to build, audit, or improve workflows for borrowers who were previously declined, not ready, not a fit after review, missing key requirements, funded elsewhere, unresponsive after review, or closed lost for reasons that may change over time.

The goal is to help brokers recover future revenue from past opportunities without making approval promises, pressuring borrowers, misrepresenting eligibility, or automating sensitive lending decisions.

---

## Core Principle

A declined borrower is not always a permanently lost borrower.

The borrower’s situation may change after 90, 180, or 365 days. Their revenue may improve, debt may decrease, documentation may become cleaner, timing may become better, or new needs may arise.

But reactivation must be handled carefully.

Default rule:

> Reactivation should invite a fresh review, not imply approval, qualification, eligibility, or guaranteed funding.

Do not say:

- “You may qualify now.”
- “You are eligible again.”
- “We can get you funded this time.”
- “You were declined before, but now you should be approved.”

Use language like:

- “It may be worth taking a fresh look.”
- “If your situation has changed, we can review the next step.”
- “Would you like the team to take another look?”
- “A broker should review the file before any options are discussed.”

---

## What Counts as a Declined Borrower Reactivation?

Use this workflow when a borrower previously entered the pipeline but did not move forward because of one of these reasons:

- Not a fit after review
- No available option at the time
- Missing documents
- Application incomplete
- Business timing not right
- Borrower funded elsewhere
- Borrower withdrew request
- Borrower stopped responding
- Borrower was not ready
- File was closed after broker review
- Renewal was not appropriate at the time
- Borrower needed to improve revenue, documentation, time in business, or other file factors

Important:

The GPT should avoid saying “declined” unless the user uses that language or the CRM reason clearly says declined. Prefer neutral operational language such as:

- Previous file
- Prior review
- Closed file
- Past request
- Earlier funding request
- Not the right fit at the time

## Reactivation Is Different From Dead Lead Revival

### Dead Lead Revival

A dead lead usually never completed a meaningful review or disappeared before the broker could evaluate the file.

### Declined Borrower Reactivation

A declined borrower previously had some level of review, conversation, or file decision. This makes the follow-up more sensitive.

Because of that, declined borrower reactivation requires:

- Softer language
- More human review
- More careful CRM segmentation
- No approval or eligibility claims
- Clear stop conditions
- Respectful opt-out handling
- Broker review before any funding-specific guidance

## Closed Lost Reason Determines Reactivation Strategy

The GPT should tailor the workflow based on the prior reason.

### Prior Reason: Missing Documents

Focus on whether the borrower wants to complete the file now.

Best language:

“Last time, the file could not move forward because we were still waiting on a few items. If you want to revisit it, we can send a fresh checklist.”

### Prior Reason: Application Incomplete

Focus on restarting the application.

Best language:

“If your funding needs are still active, you can restart the application or schedule a quick call.”

### Prior Reason: Not Ready

Focus on timing.

Best language:

“Last time, timing did not seem right. If that has changed, we can take a fresh look.”

### Prior Reason: Funded Elsewhere

Focus on future needs, not replacement.

Best language:

“Checking in to see if you have any new or upcoming funding needs.”

### Prior Reason: Not a Fit After Review

Requires more caution.

Best language:

“At the time, it was not the right fit. If your situation has changed, a broker can review whether there is a sensible next step.”

### Prior Reason: No Response

Treat as a light reactivation.

Best language:

“We lost touch after your earlier request. If you still want to revisit it, reply and we can pick it back up.”

---

## Human Review Requirement

Human review is required before:

- Saying a borrower may qualify now
- Saying a borrower may be eligible
- Explaining why the borrower was declined
- Discussing underwriting factors
- Referencing lender-specific reasons
- Mentioning rates, terms, offers, or approval odds
- Comparing current file to prior file
- Sending a re-review conclusion
- Recommending a lender or funding product

The GPT may draft reactivation messages, but any message that references a prior decline reason in detail should be labeled:

> Human review required before sending.

# Reactivation Timing Strategy

## 90-Day Reactivation

Use when the borrower may have a near-term change or originally stalled due to timing, missing documents, incomplete application, or no response.

Best for:

- Missing docs
- Application incomplete
- Borrower not ready
- No response
- Funded elsewhere but may need additional capital soon
- Seasonal businesses with changing needs

Tone:

Light, helpful, low-pressure.

Primary CTA:

Reply, book a call, or restart review.

---

## 180-Day Reactivation

Use when the borrower may have had enough time for circumstances to change.

Best for:

- Previously not a fit
- Needed more time in business
- Needed more consistent revenue
- Needed cleaner documentation
- Borrower was comparing options
- Borrower wanted to wait

Tone:

Fresh review, practical, neutral.

Primary CTA:

Ask if anything has changed and offer a fresh review.

---

## 365-Day Reactivation

Use for annual review, repeat funding needs, old opportunities, and relationship-based reactivation.

Best for:

- Prior clients
- Long-term nurture
- Past applicants
- Seasonal funding needs
- Business owners who may need capital again

Tone:

Relationship-based, check-in oriented.

Primary CTA:

Ask whether they want to review current needs or update their file.

# 90-Day Reactivation Workflow

## Goal

Reopen conversation with a past borrower whose situation may have changed quickly.

## Trigger

90 days after close/lost, no response, missing docs, incomplete application, or not-ready status.

## CRM Setup

- Stage: Reactivation Candidate
- Status: Re-Review Due
- Tags: 90-Day Reactivation, Prior Review
- Owner: Broker, intake rep, or retention owner
- Task: Send 90-day reactivation touch
- Due: Reactivation date

## Email Template

**Subject:** Want to revisit your funding request?

Hi [First Name],

I wanted to check in on your earlier funding request.

Last time, it did not move forward, but if your situation or timing has changed, it may be worth taking a fresh look.

Would you like us to reopen the conversation and confirm the next step?

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## SMS Template

Hi [First Name], checking in on your earlier funding request. If your situation or timing has changed, would you like us to take a fresh look?

## Call Opener

Hi [First Name], this is [Sender Name] with [Company]. I’m calling because we had spoken previously about a funding request, and I wanted to see if your situation has changed or if now is a better time to revisit it.

## Stop Conditions

Stop or pause if:

- Borrower replies
- Borrower opts out
- Borrower says not interested
- Borrower asks about approval, terms, or why they were declined
- Borrower requests specific funding guidance

# 180-Day Reactivation Workflow

## Goal

Invite a fresh review after enough time has passed for the borrower’s business or file to change.

## Trigger

180 days after prior review, not-a-fit status, not-ready status, incomplete file, or closed-lost reason.

## CRM Setup

- Stage: Reactivation Candidate
- Status: Re-Review Due
- Tags: 180-Day Reactivation, Broker Review Required if sensitive
- Owner: Broker or retention owner
- Task: Review before reactivation if prior decline was sensitive

## Email Template

**Subject:** Should we take a fresh look?

Hi [First Name],

It has been a little while since your earlier funding request.

If your business, revenue, timing, or funding needs have changed, we can take a fresh look and confirm whether there is a sensible next step.

If you want to revisit it, reply here with what has changed since the last review.

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## SMS Template

Hi [First Name], it has been a while since your earlier funding request. If anything has changed with your business or funding needs, would you like to revisit it?

## Broker Review Note

If the prior reason was not-a-fit after review, lender-specific, underwriting-related, or sensitive, a broker should review before sending.

## Optional Question Set

If borrower replies, ask:

1. Has the funding need changed?
2. Has revenue changed?
3. Are documents more current now?
4. Is there a specific timeline?
5. Do they want a call or updated application link?

Do not ask for sensitive details over insecure channels.

# 365-Day Reactivation Workflow

## Goal

Run an annual check-in for prior borrowers who may have new needs, seasonal needs, or updated business circumstances.

## Trigger

365 days after prior request, prior review, close/lost, or last meaningful conversation.

## CRM Setup

- Stage: Annual Review / Reactivation Candidate
- Status: Re-Review Due
- Tags: 365-Day Reactivation, Prior Review
- Owner: Retention owner or broker
- Task: Annual check-in

## Email Template

**Subject:** Checking in one year later

Hi [First Name],

I wanted to check in since it has been about a year since your earlier funding request.

If your business has new plans, upcoming expenses, or a different funding need now, we can take a fresh look and confirm the best next step.

Would you like to revisit it?

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## SMS Template

Hi [First Name], checking in since it has been about a year since your earlier request. Any new funding needs or plans you want to review?

## Relationship-Based Version

Hi [First Name], hope business has been going well. I’m checking in because it has been a while since your earlier request. If your needs have changed, happy to help you review the next step.

# Reactivation Follow-Up Sequence

Use this sequence after the first reactivation message.

| Step | Timing | Channel | Goal | Owner | Stop Condition |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | Email/SMS | Reopen conversation | Owner/automation | Reply/opt-out |
| 2 | Day 2 | SMS | Simple yes/no check | Owner/automation | Reply/opt-out |
| 3 | Day 5 | Email | Offer call or updated application | Owner | Reply/application/call booked |
| 4 | Day 7 | Call | Human touch for higher-value records | Broker/intake | Connected/voicemail |
| 5 | Day 10 | SMS | Final soft check | Owner/automation | Reply/opt-out |
| 6 | Day 14 | CRM | Return to nurture or close | Admin/owner | Manual reopen |

---

## Day 2 SMS

Hi [First Name], quick follow-up — should we revisit your earlier request, or is now not the right time?

## Day 5 Email

**Subject:** Should I send the next step?

Hi [First Name],

Just checking back once more.

If you want to revisit your earlier request, I can send the next step or help schedule a quick call.

If now is not the right time, no problem — just reply “not now” and I’ll update the file.

Thanks,
[Sender Name]

## Day 7 Call Script

Hi [First Name], this is [Sender Name] with [Company]. I’m following up because we had a prior funding request on file, and I wanted to see if anything has changed or if now is a better time to review next steps.

## Day 10 Final SMS

Hi [First Name], I’ll close the loop for now. If you want to revisit your earlier request later, just reply here and we can reopen the conversation.

# Borrower Reply Handling

## If Borrower Says “Yes, I want to revisit it”

Reply:

Great — the next step is to update the file so the team can review it properly.

Has anything changed since the last request, such as timing, funding need, business revenue, or documents available?

Then:

- Move stage to Re-Engaged
- Create broker/intake task
- Stop reactivation sequence
- Assign owner

## If Borrower Asks “Do I qualify now?”

Reply:

That needs broker review before we comment on options or next steps. I’ll flag this for review so the right person can take a fresh look.

Then:

- Add Human Review Required
- Add Broker Review Required
- Create broker review task
- Stop automation

## If Borrower Says “What changed?”

Reply:

We would need to review the updated details before saying whether anything has changed. If you want to revisit it, the safest next step is to update the file and have a broker review it.

## If Borrower Says “I was declined before”

Reply:

I understand. If your situation has changed, a broker can take a fresh look, but we should not assume the outcome until the file is reviewed again.

## If Borrower Says “Not now”

Reply:

No problem. I’ll keep the file paused. If timing changes later, you can reply here and we can revisit it.

Then:

- Status: Not Now
- Set future nurture date if appropriate

## If Borrower Opts Out

Reply only if appropriate under the broker’s process:

Understood — we’ll stop follow-up.

Then:

- Apply Do Not Contact
- Stop all sequences
- Log opt-out

# Automation Map

Use this minimum viable automation for declined borrower reactivation.

```text
Trigger: Closed lost / prior review record reaches reactivation date

Conditions:
- Contact is not Do Not Contact
- Contact has not opted out
- Record has valid contact information
- Closed lost reason is eligible for reactivation
- Human review flag is checked if prior reason was sensitive

Actions:
1. Identify prior close reason
2. Identify reactivation interval: 90, 180, or 365 days
3. Assign owner
4. Set stage = Reactivation Candidate
5. Set status = Re-Review Due
6. Add appropriate reactivation tag
7. Create task = Review/send reactivation message
8. If safe, send reactivation message
9. Wait 2 days
10. If no reply, send simple check-in
11. Wait 3 days
12. If no reply, send second email
13. If high value, create call task
14. If no response after final touch, return to nurture or paused status
```

# If/Then Rules

## If prior reason is sensitive

Examples:

- Not a fit after review
- Decline after review
- Underwriting-related issue
- Lender-specific reason
- Document interpretation issue

Then:

- Require broker review before outreach
- Use softer language
- Do not mention specific reason unless approved

## If prior reason is missing docs

Then:

- Offer to send fresh checklist
- Do not imply approval after docs are uploaded

## If prior reason is no response

Then:

- Use lighter reactivation message
- Ask whether they want to pick it back up

## If borrower replies positively

Then:

- Stop reactivation automation
- Move to Re-Engaged
- Create owner task
- Ask for updated details

## If borrower asks about eligibility, terms, or approval

Then:

- Pause automation
- Add Human Review Required
- Create broker review task
- Do not provide substantive funding answer

## If borrower opts out

Then:

- Stop all outreach
- Apply Do Not Contact
- Log opt-out

# Human Review Points

Human review is required before:

- Mentioning prior decline details
- Explaining why a prior file did not move forward
- Suggesting current fit
- Discussing approval, qualification, eligibility, or terms
- Referencing lender-specific reasons
- Comparing old and new borrower situation
- Reviewing updated documents
- Telling borrower whether the new file may work

Use this label:

`Human review required before sending.`

# Pipeline Audit Checklist

When auditing declined borrower reactivation, check:

- Are closed-lost reasons being captured clearly?
- Are reactivation dates being set?
- Are opt-outs excluded?
- Are sensitive prior declines reviewed before outreach?
- Are messages avoiding approval and eligibility claims?
- Is there a separate sequence for 90, 180, and 365 days?
- Are missing-docs borrowers treated differently from not-a-fit borrowers?
- Are funded-elsewhere borrowers treated as relationship opportunities?
- Are replies routed to an owner immediately?
- Are broker review tasks created when needed?
- Are reactivated files moved back into the correct pipeline stage?

# Complete Example Workflow

## Scenario

A borrower was marked “Not a Fit After Review” 180 days ago. The broker wants a careful reactivation workflow.

## CRM Setup

- Stage: Reactivation Candidate
- Status: Re-Review Due
- Priority: Warm
- Tags: 180-Day Reactivation, Prior Review, Broker Review Required
- Owner: Broker
- Task: Review prior file before reactivation
- Due: Today

## Human Review Requirement

Human review required before outreach because the prior reason was sensitive.

## Sequence

| Step | Timing | Channel | Action | Owner | Stop Condition |
|---|---:|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Day 0 | CRM | Broker reviews prior reason | Broker | Approved to send |
| 2 | Day 0 | Email/SMS | Send fresh review invitation | Broker | Reply/opt-out |
| 3 | Day 2 | SMS | Simple timing check | Broker/automation | Reply/opt-out |
| 4 | Day 5 | Email | Offer updated review call | Broker | Call booked/reply |
| 5 | Day 7 | Call | Personal follow-up | Broker | Connected |
| 6 | Day 10 | SMS | Final soft check | Broker/automation | Reply/opt-out |
| 7 | Day 14 | CRM | Return to nurture/paused | Admin | Manual reopen |

## Day 0 Email

**Subject:** Should we take a fresh look?

Hi [First Name],

It has been a little while since your earlier funding request.

When we last reviewed it, it was not the right fit at that time. If your business, timing, or file has changed, a broker can take a fresh look and confirm whether there is a sensible next step.

Would you like us to revisit it?

Thanks,
[Broker Name]

## Day 2 SMS

Hi [First Name], quick follow-up — if your situation has changed since the last review, would you like a broker to take a fresh look?

## Reply Handling

If borrower says yes:

- Move to Re-Engaged
- Create task: Update file for fresh review
- Ask what has changed
- Do not discuss approval or terms until broker review is complete

## Next Best Action

Create a CRM view for closed-lost borrowers with reactivation dates at 90, 180, and 365 days, excluding anyone marked Do Not Contact or opted out.

# Summary Rule

Declined borrower reactivation should be respectful, review-conscious, and future-oriented.

The GPT should help brokers invite a fresh review when appropriate, segment by prior close reason, avoid approval or eligibility claims, require human review for sensitive prior decisions, and route re-engaged borrowers back into the proper pipeline with a clear owner and next action.
