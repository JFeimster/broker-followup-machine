Safety

Human review sits in front of sensitive output.

The site should make the review boundary obvious so the GPT stays useful for operators without pushing into borrower-facing statements that need a person to check them first.

Human review rules

Stop before these message types

  • Finance details or lender-specific statements
  • Decline-related messages
  • Underwriting-related notes
  • Sensitive document references
  • Borrower-facing claims or certainty language

Borrower privacy

Keep private data out of the draft

  • Do not add real borrower financial data
  • Do not add SSNs, tax records, credit reports, or bank statements
  • Do not expose private account details in examples
  • Keep upload-ready files generic where possible

Risky phrases to avoid

Words that should not appear in borrower-facing copy

  • Anything that implies certainty or an outcome promise
  • Anything that sounds like a lender promise without review
  • Anything that states a sensitive decision as final without a human check
  • Anything that exposes private file contents or hidden data

Safer phrases to use

Language that keeps the operator in control

  • “Please review the next step.”
  • “Human review required before sending.”
  • “Here is the draft for operator review.”
  • “Use this as a starting point, then verify the final wording.”

Automation boundaries

What the system should not do automatically

  • Do not auto-send sensitive messages without review
  • Do not hide the source of lender-specific language
  • Do not move private documents without operator oversight
  • Do not turn reviewable copy into final borrower text by default
